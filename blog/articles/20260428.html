1st: Ζ# (@Zeta_Sharp)

The 1st one is me. My pick is Ingenuity Slim by Parker. Parker 5th technology, a new writing mode is inside. Simply saying, it's luxury fine writer. Apparently, the flexible resin tip is guided by an engraved metallic hood.

A metal body painted in matte black accented by a blue ring. The iconic arrow clip features a sharper design to match the slim body.

Above all, the machining precision is amazing, with absolutely seamless transition between the section and barrel.

I love this pen.

haya's pick is Pilot Automatic.

This is a discontinued mechanical pencil by Pilot. The black base peeks through from beneath the silver outer parts, and the grip is etched and slightly recessed.

Combined with its internal mechanics, the design feels highly industrial and inorganic, yet carries a touch of nostalgia.

Asou's pick is Sailor rencontre, 3rd limited model Terre.

The deep marble pattern—reminiscent of the ocean that covers 70% of the Earth's surface—is absolutely mesmerizing.

The marble patterns are a product of chance during the injection molding process, making each one truly unique. It is literally a once-in-a-lifetime "rencontre" (encounter).

It looks stunning enough in photos, so I can only imagine how incredible it must be to hold it in person. I feel like I might get sucked into the bottomless swamp of marble patterns.

ぐりぶん's pick is Nicholas Hemingway Wooden-body mechanical pencil.

It features a slim, straight, and minimalist body, stripping away all ornamentation except for a subtle logo. This simplicity allows the rich texture of the wood grain to truly stand out. The metal fittings and the wooden body harmonize perfectly, creating a serene, beautiful pen.

朧 vioel 永遠's pick is Pineider La Grande Bellezza, limited color arco violet。(Is this the correct way to separate it...?)

The arch of the clip and the curve of the grip are incredibly well-crafted. I'm particularly interested in the window-like design between the section and the barrel.
